Week 2 · Comparing
⚖️ Comparing Decimals — The Big Number Illusion
Dora says: "0.09 is bigger than 0.1 because 9 is bigger than 1."
Is she right?
0.1 = 10/100
vs
0.09 = 9/100
0.1 = 10 hundredths. 0.09 = 9 hundredths. So 0.1 > 0.09.
🧠 The Invisible Zero trick:

When comparing 0.1 and 0.09, write 0.1 as 0.10 (tens of hundredths). Now they have the same number of decimal places: 0.10 vs 0.09. Easy — 10 > 9.

Week 4 · Rounding
🎯 Rounding to the Nearest Whole
Look at the tenths digit:
• 0–4 → round DOWN (stay)
• 5–9 → round UP (jump up)
This is the "High Five Rule" — 5 always jumps up. ✋
Round to the nearest whole:
⚠️ Tricky case: 0.4 rounds to 0 (not to nothing — 0 IS the nearest whole). And 3.5 rounds to 4 (the High Five Rule).
Week 4 · Application
🏘️ Decimal of a Quantity — Housing Estate
There are 100 houses on a new estate.
To find a decimal of a quantity: turn the decimal into a fraction over 100, then count the squares.
0.24 of 100 houses have 3 bedrooms = 24 houses
📐 The formula (works for any whole):

Decimal of a quantity = (decimal as a fraction) ÷ denominator × numerator × quantity
Easier: 0.24 = 24100. So 0.24 of 100 = 100 ÷ 100 × 24 = 24. ✅

Volume 2 · Key Equivalents
🍕 ¼, ½ and ¾ as Decimals — MUST-KNOW
Three fractions appear in every Year 4 paper. You must know their decimals by heart.
Click each card to reveal.
¼ = 0.25  ·  ½ = 0.5 (or 0.50)  ·  ¾ = 0.75
🔁 Why?

A whole = 100 hundredths. Split into 4 equal parts → each is 100 ÷ 4 = 25 hundredths = 0.25.
So ¼ = 25/100 = 0.25 → ½ = 50/100 = 0.50 → ¾ = 75/100 = 0.75.
